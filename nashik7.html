Harihar Fort

Harihar Fort, locally known as Harshgad or Harihargad, has roots steeped deep in history. Believed to have been constructed during the Yadava dynasty in the 11th century, the fort stands as a testament to the strategic prowess and cultural richness of ancient India. Harihar Fort is located about 48 kilometers away from Nashik, making it accessible for both local and international tourists. The nearest village, Nirgudpada, acts as the base for trekkers.
