Gawilgadh Fort

Perched high in the Satpura Range, Gawilgad Fort stands as a formidable testament to Maharashtra’s rich tapestry of history and architectural brilliance. This ancient stronghold, with its imposing walls and strategic vantage points, has witnessed centuries of battles and dynastic changes and has played a pivotal role in shaping the region’s destiny. The origins of Gawilgad Fort trace back to the 12th century, when it is believed to have been a mud structure built by a local Gawali king. The fort’s name is derived from the Gawli community, traditional cowherds who inhabited the Berar region.
