Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort, originally known as Rairi, was captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1656 from the Chandraraoji More dynasty and renamed Raigad, meaning "royal fort". It became the capital of the Maratha Empire, and Shivaji was crowned here on June 6, 1674, marking the foundation of Hindavi Swarajya. The fort later came under Mughal, Siddi, and British control, earning the nickname "Gibraltar of the East" due to its steep, nearly impregnable terrain.
